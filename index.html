Biography

Astad Dinshah Gorwala (1902–1981) was an influential Indian journalist, author, and social reformer known for his insightful political commentary and commitment to justice. Born into a Parsi family in Bombay, he developed a passion for civic issues and responsible governance early in life.

Over the decades, Gorwala became known for his bold editorials in The Times of India and The Statesman, where he fearlessly critiqued British colonial policies and later, post-independence political excesses. His writings became a voice of reason in times of uncertainty.

"The true measure of a society is found in how it treats its most vulnerable members."

He authored several respected books such as "The Role of the Administrator" and "Report on Public Administration", emphasizing transparency, accountability, and the need for ethical governance in a democratic India.

Gorwala's work earned him admiration from both peers and future reformers. He continued writing until his final years, leaving behind a legacy marked by courage, intellect, and unwavering moral integrity.
